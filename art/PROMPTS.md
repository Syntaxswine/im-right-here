# the plates

Six illustrations commissioned for the piece, 2026-07-06. Originals live in this
directory at full weight (the plates); the story loads the web derivatives in
`img/` (regenerate with `tools\derive-art.ps1`). Generation prompts, verbatim:

## 1. hush — the opening dark

```
A dark empty room, single bare bulb hanging from cracked plaster ceiling, shadows
swallowing the corners, a low hum visualized as faint vibrations in the air like
heat waves, surrealist dada atmosphere, ink and watercolor texture, muted ochre
and deep umber --raw --ar 2:3
```

## 2. ear — the listening

```
Profile of a person pressing their ear against a textured wall in total darkness,
the wall breathes like the flank of a sleeping animal, voices visualized as thin
threads of pale light weaving through plaster cracks, intimate, surreal, muted
colors --raw --ar 2:3
```

## 3. corridor — five doors

```
Endless surreal corridor floating in darkness, five doors arranged like a stage
set: one knocking on itself, one with a hat on its knob, one with a spotlight
bleeding under it, one nailed with a manifesto, one that looks like a face,
dadaist dreamlike, washed out colors, ink wash --raw --ar 2:3
```

## 4. found — the meeting in darkness

```
Two figures meeting in total darkness, nothing visible but warm amber light
between them shaped exactly like a listening ear, the dark itself is warm and
welcoming, abstract emotional surrealist, soft edges, ink wash texture
--raw --ar 2:3
```

## 5. cabaret — performing your name

```
Tiny stage the size of a coin, empty spotlight burning patient as a hole in
night's pocket, a hat full of cut-out letters, dada cabaret atmosphere, surreal,
muted spotlight against deep black --raw --ar 2:3
```

## 6. rehearsal — behind the wall

```
Behind a wall, three shadow figures rehearsing in dim light, pipes full of
borrowed weather, voices whispering "too soon, it hasn't got the name yet",
surreal backstage, ink and watercolor, muted --raw --ar 2:3
```

(The rehearsal plate came back with the passage's own lines painted onto the
wall — "too soon… hasn't got the name yet… borrowed weather" — which is the
walls quoting the script back at the production. Kept, obviously.)
